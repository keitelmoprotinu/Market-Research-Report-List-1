<p><p>The surgical vacuum regulator market is categorized into two main types: analog and digital vacuum regulators. Analog vacuum regulators operate using mechanical components and provide continuous vacuum control through a dial or gauge, offering reliability and simplicity. In contrast, digital vacuum regulators utilize electronic controls for precise vacuum measurements and adjustments, often featuring digital displays and advanced monitoring capabilities. Both types are essential in surgical settings for maintaining optimal suction levels during procedures, ensuring patient safety and effective operation.</p></p>
