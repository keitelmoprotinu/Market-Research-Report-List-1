The polarographic type utilizes an electrode that measures current produced by oxygen reduction, offering rapid and accurate readings. In contrast, primary battery type dissolved oxygen meters employ a built-in battery to power the sensor, making them portable and user-friendly for fieldwork. Each type serves specific needs, balancing accuracy, ease of use, and measurement environments in the market.</p></p>
