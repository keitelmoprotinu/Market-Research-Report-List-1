<p><p>The Internet of Nano Things (IoNT) encompasses various communication types based on distance. Short-distance communication focuses on connecting nano-scale devices within close proximity, typically using technologies like Bluetooth or Zigbee, ideal for applications in smart health monitoring or industrial automation. In contrast, long-distance communication enables devices to connect over greater ranges using protocols like LoRaWAN or cellular networks, facilitating remote monitoring and control in sectors such as agriculture and environmental sensing, thus enhancing data accessibility and operational efficiency.</p></p>
